Nandgaon

The village of Nanda Baba — where Krishna was truly a son of the soil

Nandgaon is the village of Nanda Maharaj — the foster father who raised Krishna with Yashoda Maiya in the pastoral bliss of the Braj countryside. Located on a hill just 8 km from Barsana, Nandgaon is forever connected to Barsana through the playful rivalry of their Lathmar Holi — Krishna's boys versus Radha's girls. The hill, the temple, and the surrounding fields carry a deep rural warmth.

Must-Visit Places in Nandgaon

Nanda Bhavan Mandir (Nand Ji Temple)

The magnificent hilltop temple of Nandgaon — housing deities of Nanda Maharaj, Yashoda Maiya, Balaram, and Bal Krishna. The view from the hilltop — particularly toward Barsana in the distance — is magical and deeply evocative of the Braj countryside that Krishna called home.

Climb the hill in early morning when it's cool and the light falls golden on the plains below.

Lathmar Holi — Nandgaon Style

The men of Nandgaon go to Barsana one day and get playfully beaten with lathis. The following day, Barsana's women come to Nandgaon and the roles reverse. The entire Nandgaon erupts in colour, bhajans, and joyful chaos. The Nand Ji temple becomes the epicentre of celebrations.

Nandgaon's Holi is slightly less crowded than Barsana — a better choice for those who want a more immersive experience.

Pavana Sarovar

A sacred lake connected to Nanda Maharaj's daily devotion — he would bathe here and offer prayers toward the Yamuna. A peaceful, contemplative kund surrounded by old temples and trees — much less visited and deeply serene.

Uddhava Kyari

The garden where Uddhava — Krishna's dear friend and messenger — came to deliver Krishna's message to the gopis and residents of Vraja. The gopis' response to Uddhava became the famous "Bhramara Geet" (Song of the Bumblebee) — one of the most beautiful passages in the entire Bhagavatam.

Ter Kadamb

A Kadamba tree atop a hill near Nandgaon where Krishna would climb and call his cows home with his flute in the evenings. The site retains an extraordinary pastoral silence — listening to the wind here, you can almost hear the flute echoing across the Braj plains.
