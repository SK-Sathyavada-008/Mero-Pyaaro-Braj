The most beloved temple of Vrindavan, housing the deity manifest from Nidhivan by Swami Haridas ji. The deity of Banke Bihari is mesmerising ‚Äî Krishna in his playful three-bent posture with his peacock feather. The parda (curtain) is raised and lowered every few seconds during darshan, as the priests believe no devotee can bear the full force of his gaze for long.

The Shringar aarti during Kartik is very special and rare ‚Äî check timings in advance.

Nidhivan

A mysterious, sacred grove where the trees are permanently twisted and intertwined. It is believed that Radha-Krishna perform their Maharaas here every night. The grove is locked before sunset ‚Äî no one is allowed inside after dark. Even the birds fly away as dusk falls. A profoundly sacred and deeply mysterious place.

Do not enter after sunset ‚Äî it is strictly forbidden, upheld for centuries. Visit before 5 PM.

Seva Kunj

The sacred grove where the Maharaas ‚Äî the divine dance of Krishna with the gopis ‚Äî is said to have taken place. During Sharad Purnima night, this becomes the spiritual centre of all Vrindavan. Ancient trees, winding paths, and a deep stillness pervade this space.

Radha Damodar Mandir

One of Vrindavan's oldest and most scholarly temples, established by Jeeva Goswami ‚Äî a direct disciple of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. The samadhi (tomb) of Rupa Goswami is here. Also the bhajan kutir of Srila Prabhupada. A deeply sacred place for all Vaishnavas worldwide.

Ideal for sitting quietly, reading, or just being in stillness. The atmosphere is unlike anywhere else.

Govind Dev Ji Mandir

Built in 1590 by Raja Man Singh of Amber ‚Äî once the tallest temple in Vrindavan with seven stories. Aurangzeb demolished the upper floors, but the remaining structure is still magnificent. The Govinda deity was installed by Rupa Goswami himself using Krishna's own instructions.

Keshi Ghat

The ghat where Krishna slew the demon Keshi who had taken the form of a terrible horse. The Yamuna flows peacefully here today ‚Äî wide, calm, and golden at sunset. One of the most atmospheric and photogenic spots in all of Vrindavan.

Radha Raman Mandir

Among the most authentic temples in Vrindavan ‚Äî the deity of Radha Raman manifested from a shaligram shila for Gopala Bhatta Goswami. The original deity has been worshipped in an unbroken lineage by the same Goswami family since 1542. No replica has ever been made.

The fragrance of fresh flowers and the simplicity of traditional seva here is unlike anything else in Vrindavan.

Vanshi Vat

The ancient Kadamba tree on the Yamuna bank where Krishna played his flute. The gopis, upon hearing the flute, left everything and came running. This spot marks the beginning of the Maharaas ‚Äî one of the most intimate and reverent places in all of Braj.

ISKCON Vrindavan (Krishna Balaram Mandir)

A magnificent white marble temple complex founded by Srila Prabhupada, with deities of Krishna-Balaram, Radha-Shyamasundar, and Gaura-Nitai. The evening Gaura aarti is one of the most joyful spiritual events in Vrindavan ‚Äî the entire hall erupts in kirtan.

Madan Mohan Mandir

Vrindavan's oldest and most historically significant temple, perched on a small hill overlooking the Yamuna. The deity was installed by Sanatana Goswami. The setting ‚Äî with peacocks, the Yamuna glittering below, and the sound of bells ‚Äî is dreamlike.

Prem Mandir

A breathtaking white marble temple built by Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj, completed in 2012. The entire structure is carved from Italian marble with intricate scenes of Krishna's leelas covering every wall and pillar. At night, the temple is lit up in changing colours ‚Äî making it one of the most visually stunning sights in all of Vrindavan.

Visit after sunset for the light-and-colour display ‚Äî the illuminated marble glows beautifully against the dark sky.
