Govardhan

Giriraj — the sacred hill Krishna lifted on his little finger to shelter all of Braj

Govardhan Hill — called Giriraj ("King of Mountains") — is one of the most sacred sites in all of Hinduism. Krishna lifted this hill with his little finger to shelter the Brajwasis from Indra's torrential wrath. Every stone of Giriraj is worshipped as a form of Krishna himself. The 21-km Govardhan Parikrama is among the most powerful devotional acts a pilgrim can undertake in Braj.

Must-Visit Places at Govardhan

Govardhan Hill (Giriraj)

Giriraj Govardhan is worshipped as a directly manifested form of Krishna. Every stone on the hill is offered milk, water, and flowers by devotees. The hill is said to be slowly sinking into the earth — a few centimetres every hundred years — as it patiently awaits reunion with the divine.

Pick up a small stone from the ground (not from the hill) as prasad — called "Giriraj shila" — a sacred memento of your visit.

Radha Kund & Shyam Kund

The two most sacred kunds in all of Braj. Radha Kund was created by Radha Rani herself; Shyam Kund was created by Krishna. Bathing here on Bahula Ashtami (Kartik month) is considered the highest spiritual act in all of Braj. Even sitting quietly at the edge at dawn is profoundly moving.

Mukharavind

The most important temple on the Govardhan parikrama — Mukharavind is the "face" (mukha) of Giriraj, a natural indentation in the hill that resembles a face. Thousands of pilgrims offer milk, flowers, and full-body prostrations here every single day.

Mansi Ganga

A sacred lake at the heart of Govardhan town — created from Krishna's mind (manas). Krishna wanted to bring the Ganga here so the Brajwasis wouldn't need to travel far. Bathing here is considered equivalent to bathing in the Ganga. Especially beautiful during Kartik Purnima with floating lamps.

Daan Ghat

Where Krishna would playfully demand dan (toll/tax) from Radha and the gopis as they carried pots of milk and curd. The joyful dialogue between Krishna and Radha at this spot is celebrated in countless Braj bhajans. A site filled with leela memory and joy.

Kusum Sarovar

A stunningly beautiful ornamental stepwell-lake between Govardhan and Radha Kund. Radha would gather flowers (kusums) here from the surrounding gardens. The carved ghats and old stone pavilions make this one of the most visually magnificent spots in all of Braj.

Kusum Sarovar at sunset is extraordinary — golden light on the water, lotus blooms, ancient carved stone. Don't miss it.
