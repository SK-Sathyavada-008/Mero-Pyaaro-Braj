Gokul

Where Bal Krishna grew up — Nanda Maharaj's village of cowherds and divine mischief

Gokul is where newborn Krishna was secretly carried by his father Vasudeva across the flooded Yamuna on that stormy Janmashtami night — and placed in the care of Nanda and Yashoda. It is here that Krishna spent his earliest years as Bal Gopal — crawling in the dust, stealing butter, and hiding in Yashoda's lap. The town retains a quiet, village-like sanctity that feels truly timeless.

Must-Visit Places in Gokul

Nanda Bhavan

The home of Nanda Maharaj and Mother Yashoda — where Krishna was raised as their son. The site has a beautiful temple with Bal Krishna's darshan. The courtyard evokes the image of baby Krishna crawling between the grinding stones, butter pots on high shelves, and Yashoda chasing the little thief with a rope.

The deity of Bal Gopal here is especially tender — a favourite for devotees who see Krishna as their own child.

Brahmanda Ghat

The sacred ghat where the Brahmanda leela occurred — young Krishna ate mud, and when Yashoda asked him to open his mouth, she saw the entire universe within. A deeply philosophical leela made visible in the simplest act of childhood. Evening aarti here is serene.

Yashoda Kund

The sacred kund where Yashoda bathed baby Krishna. Near here is also the site of the ukhala bandhana leela — where Yashoda tied Krishna to the grinding stone, which then dragged along and knocked down the twin Yamala-Arjuna trees, liberating two celestial beings trapped within. A tender site.

Vasudeva Crossing Point

The spot where Vasudeva is believed to have crossed the flooding Yamuna with newborn Krishna on the night of Janmashtami — balancing the basket above the raging river while Sheshnag sheltered them from the storm. A deeply evocative spot that brings scripture to life.

Raman Reti

The sacred sandy grounds just outside Gokul where Krishna and Balaram played as children with the other cowherds. "Raman Reti" means "sand of joy." Walking barefoot on this sand is considered a purifying act — many devotees even roll on it in devotion.

Remove your footwear and walk slowly in the sand — there is something quietly powerful about this place.

Gokul Chandrama Temple

A beautiful, peaceful temple on the Yamuna bank dedicated to the combined form of Radha-Krishna in their Gokul leelas. Less crowded than the main spots — perfect for quiet darshan and reflection.
